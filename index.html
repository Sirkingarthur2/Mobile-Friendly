Apostle Paul

Who is Apostle Paul?

Apostle Paul, originally known as Saul of Tarsus, was a known Jew and pharisee. He was going to Damascus, and dedicated his life to being Christian after an encounter with Jesus left him blind for three days.

What makes apostle Paul so great?

Apostle Paul wrote almost half of New Testament of the Bible. He is also considered one of the most influential christians of all time, because of his many works.
